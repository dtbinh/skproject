Piranha Front Panel

This topic provides information on the hardware features available via the front panel interface that are specific to the RX6 Piranha Processor. The RX6 front panel includes the LCD Display and Status Lights common to all high performance processors. For more information see Status Displays. For hardware specifications see the Piranha Technical Specifications.

Analog Input/Output

The RX6 has two channels of 24-bit, sigma-delta D/A and two channels of 24-bit, sigma-delta A/D, each accessible through BNC connectors. This device can sample at rates up to 260 kHz for a realizable bandwidth of 120 kHz. For specific information on the actual sampling rates see Realizable Sampling Rates for the RX6

Important Note!: The RX6's sigma-delta A/D inputs are accessed as ADC channels 128 and 129, because the RX6 can also acquire analog signals using a preamplifier via an optional fiber optic port.

Digital I/O

The Digital I/O circuits include 24 bits of programmable I/O in two eight bit word-addressable bytes and eight bits of bit-addressable I/O. Digital I/O lines are accessed via the 25-pin connector on the front panel and can be configured as inputs or outputs. See the Digital Input/Output Configuration topic for more information.

Caution!: The first eight bits of bit-addressable digital I/O on RX devices are unbuffered. When used as inputs, overvoltages on these lines can cause severe damage to the system. TDT recommends when sending digital signals into the device, (1) never send a signal with amplitude greater than seven volts into any digital input and (2) always use the byte-addressable digital I/O lines.

Bits Lights

The eight Bits lights are user configurable. By default the Bits lights indicate the logic level (light when high) for the eight bit-addressable digital I/O lines. The Bits lights can also be configured to provide information amplifier status or act as logic level lights for the other two bytes of Digital I/O. See the Digital Input/Output Configuration topic for more information on configuring the lights.

Amp Lights

The single optical light (for the optical port) indicates the power status of the connected amplifier.  When an amplifier is not connected the light will flash in a slow steady pattern. The light is lit when the amplifier is connected and begins to flash quickly when the voltage on the battery for the amplifier is low. The Li-ion batteries voltage decreases rapidly once the battery low light pattern begins. The signal will become noisy if the battery is not charged soon after the fast flash pattern begins. See Amp Status and Clip Warning Lights for more information.

XLink

The XLink is not supported at this time.

Fiber Optic Port (Optional)

The base station acquires digitized signals from the Medusa preamplifier over a fiber optic cable. This provides loss-less signal acquisition between the amplifier and the base station on up to 16 channels at a maximum sampling rate of 25 kHz. The port provides oversampling. See Fiber Oversampling for more information. The fiber optic port can be used with any of the Medusa preamplifiers including, the RA16PA and the RA4PA, or the RA8GA. The channels are numbered 1 - 16.

When an amplifier is not connected the adjacent amp (fiber optic port) light will flash in a slow steady pattern. The light is lit when the amplifier is connected and begins to flash quickly when the voltage on the battery for the corresponding amplifier is low. The Li-ion batteries voltage decreases rapidly once the battery low light pattern begins. The signal will become noisy if the battery is not charged soon after the fast flash pattern begins.
